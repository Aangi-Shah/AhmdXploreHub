Kankaria Lake

Kankaria Lake is a historic and artificial lake situated in the heart of Ahmedabad, the largest city in the Indian state of Gujarat. The lake, constructed in the 15th century by Sultan Qutb-ud-Din, was initially intended for irrigation purposes and later developed into a multi-faceted recreational space. Covering an area of about 34 acres, Kankaria Lake has undergone significant transformations over the centuries.

The lake area features a beautifully landscaped promenade, known as the Kankaria Carnival, which includes gardens, a zoo, an amusement park called "Balvatika," and an aquarium. The lakefront is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, offering a variety of activities such as boat rides, toy train rides, and balloon safaris.

Adalaj Stepwell

The Adalaj Stepwell, constructed in 1499 near Ahmedabad, Gujarat, is a stunning example of medieval Indian architecture. Commissioned by Queen Rudabai, the stepwell served both as a functional water storage system and a masterpiece of artistic expression. Today, the Adalaj Stepwell stands as a well-preserved heritage site, attracting visitors with its unique blend of utility and aesthetic appeal.

Science City

Science City is a popular science and education center located in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India. It is an interactive and dynamic space designed to promote scientific awareness, education, and curiosity among visitors of all ages. The project was initiated by the government of Gujarat and developed by the Gujarat Council of Science City. It plays a crucial role in promoting scientific temperament, fostering curiosity, and encouraging the pursuit of knowledge in the field of science and technology.
