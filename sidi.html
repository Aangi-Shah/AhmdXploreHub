Sidi Saiyyed Ni Jali

A mesmerizing lattice masterpiece showcasing exquisite craftsmanship in Ahmedabad.

About

Sidi Saiyyed ni Jali refers to the intricately carved lattice window located in the Sidi Saiyyed Mosque in Ahmedabad, India. Constructed in 1573, this mosque is known for its exquisite architectural design, with the highlight being the stunning jali (lattice) work on the western wall.

The Sidi Saiyyed ni Jali is particularly famous for its fine mesh of stone filigree, featuring a tree-like pattern with intertwining branches and foliage. This delicate and artistic craftsmanship exemplifies the rich cultural and architectural heritage of Ahmedabad, making it a notable landmark in the city.

Key Features

The mosque itself is a historical structure built in 1573. It is a small but elegant mosque that reflects the architectural styles of its time.

  • Tree of Life Motif
  • Architectural Heritage
