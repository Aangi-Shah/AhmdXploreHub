Gandhi Ashram

A Living Testament to Mahatma Gandhi

About

Gandhi Ashram, also known as Sabarmati Ashram, is a historical site located in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India. Established by Mahatma Gandhi in 1917, it served as the headquarters for the Indian independence movement.

The ashram is situated on the banks of the Sabarmati River and was Mahatma Gandhi's residence for over a decade. Today, it stands as a museum and a memorial to Gandhi's life and teachings.

Visitors can explore the ashram to learn about Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence (Ahimsa) and his role in India's struggle for independence. The serene surroundings and exhibits at the ashram offer a glimpse into the life and principles of one of the most influential figures in modern history.

Key Features

Gandhi Ashram continues to attract visitors who seek to understand and appreciate the life and legacy of one of the most influential figures in India's history with its main features:
  • Gandhi's Living Quarters
  • Museum and Exhibits
  • Hriday Kunj
  • Library
  • Vinoba Kutir
  • Magan Niwas
  • Gandhi Smarak Sangrahalaya
  • Gandhi's Office
  • Prayer Ground
